\section{Profiling Policy Selection}
\label{sec:profiling-policy-selection}

Profiling policy selection decides how much observation is justified. It
consumes cost estimates, diagnostics, risk interpretation, and an observation
budget. It does not decide final scheduling, routing, or placement.

Let $\mathcal{P}$ be a set of available policies. A selector may be written as
\[
\rho(a)
=
\operatorname*{arg\,select}_{p\in\mathcal{P}}
J_p\!\left(
\widehat{\mathbf{C}}(a),
\mathrm{Diag}(a),
\mathrm{Risk}(a),
B_{\mathrm{obs}}
\right),
\]
where $B_{\mathrm{obs}}$ is the observation budget. A stable low-risk task may
use minimal accounting. A high-tail or high-residual task may justify detailed
accounting, tracing, or diagnostic profiling.
